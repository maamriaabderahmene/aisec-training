# Vibe or Die

**Break activity · 8 minutes · everyone builds · no judging the code**

---

## Timeline

| Time | Phase |
|------|-------|
| 0:00 – 0:30 | Reveal the prompt |
| 0:30 – 5:30 | Build it (silent) |
| 5:30 – 7:30 | Screen shares |
| 7:30 – 8:00 | Vote + winner |

---

## Step by step — what you say

**1. Set the scene (30 sec)** `hype them up`

> "We're taking an 8-minute break — but not a boring one. Everyone opens Replit or Lovable right now. You have exactly 5 minutes to generate an app from a single prompt. Ready?"

**2. Reveal the prompt (10 sec)** `paste in chat`

Drop it in the chat and read it out loud — only one prompt for everyone. Same starting line, different vibes.

**3. Start the 5-minute clock** `stay silent`

Say: "Go!" — then go silent. Put a visible countdown on screen if you can. No helping each other. Let the chaos happen. Encourage iteration: if the first gen is boring, ask the AI to make it weirder.

**4. 30-second screen shares** `30 sec each, strict`

Each person shares their screen for max 30 seconds. No explanation needed — just show the vibe. You narrate lightly: "love the colour", "bold choice", "what is happening here".

**5. Group vote + winner** `simultaneous vote`

Everyone types their vote in chat simultaneously (3, 2, 1, go). Count the names. Winner gets the title "Most Chaotic Dev" for the rest of the session. No prize needed — the title is enough.

## The only rules

✅ You can re-prompt the AI as many times as you want  
✅ Judging is on vibe only — not on quality or completeness  
✅ You can use Replit, Lovable, Bolt, v0 — anything goes  

❌ No writing code manually — AI only  
❌ No apologising for your result — own the chaos  
❌ No more than 30 seconds of screen share — strictly timed

## Voting categories

| Trophy | Category | Criteria |
|--------|----------|----------|
| 🔥 | Best vibe | Looks the most alive |
| 🌀 | Most chaotic | We don't know what this is |
| 🚀 | Most shippable | Actually kind of useful? |
| 🎨 | Best UI | Somehow looks good |

---

> **Facilitator tip:** the real goal is to show that a complete, functional-looking app can exist in 5 minutes from a single sentence. When students see their own result next to everyone else's — same prompt, wildly different outcomes — it clicks that the prompt is the product. That's the lesson, disguised as a game.
